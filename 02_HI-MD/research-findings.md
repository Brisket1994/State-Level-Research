## Executive Summary

This deliverable documents the current law, as of 2026-06-23, governing contracts for student transportation services (including school bus contracts) between public school districts and private contractors across ten states — Hawaii, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, and Maryland — covering five fields: maximum contract term, whether a rebid is required at term-end, renewal mechanics, the statutory citation, and district-level interpretation variance. The posture is that of a neutral recorder of law: each state was researched independently and then adversarially validated against its own primary statutory and administrative authorities, with every load-bearing citation re-opened on the official source. No cross-state legal inference was drawn — silence in one state's law was not filled by another's — and no state's framework is evaluated as favorable or unfavorable to any party.

## Notes / Special Provisions

### Hawaii
- **Single statewide LEA.** Hawaii is the only U.S. state with one statewide school district. All public-school student-transportation contracting is procured by the Hawaii State Department of Education ("HIDOE") and its Procurement and Contracts Branch, operating under the State Procurement Office. There is no county-by-county or board-by-board procurement variation.

- **Procurement code applies to HIDOE.** HRS section 103D-102(a) applies HRS chapter 103D to "all procurement contracts made by governmental bodies," and HRS section 103D-104 defines "Governmental body" to include "any department, commission, council, board, bureau, authority, committee, institution, legislative body, agency, government corporation, or other establishment or office of the executive, legislative, or judicial branch of the State, including the office of Hawaiian affairs, and the several counties of the State." HIDOE is a state executive department. The Governor's August 17, 2023 Proclamation Relating to School Bus Services expressly suspends HRS chapter 103D for HIDOE school-bus contracting during the school-bus-driver-shortage emergency, which presupposes that chapter 103D otherwise applies.

- **Prior school-bus-specific extension framework REPEALED in 2013.** Before Act 257, SLH 2013 (S.B. 1082), HRS section 302A-407 contained subsections expressly authorizing two-year extensions of school-bus contracts by mutual agreement (with an additional two-year extension), capped at a five-per-cent annual compensation increase plus reasonable fuel-cost adjustments, plus renegotiation procedures and a vehicle-age limit and refurbishment requirement. Act 257 (effective July 2, 2013) struck all of that framework, leaving 302A-407 with only safety-equipment/signage/standards rules (subsection (a)) and the school-bus fare revolving fund (subsection (b)). The legislative purpose recital in Act 257 expressly justified the change as giving DOE "greater flexibility in the contracting of student transportation" by routing school-bus contracting through HRS chapter 103D. Any older secondary source still describing two-year mutual extensions or 5% compensation caps as part of 302A-407 is reporting REPEALED law.

- **No current transportation-specific term cap.** As amended, HRS section 302A-407 is the only school-bus-contract-specific statute, and it does not address contract term, competitive procurement, renewal, or rebid. The contracting framework therefore defaults entirely to HRS chapter 103D.

- **Multi-term contracts explicitly contemplate school-bus contractors.** HAR section 3-122-149(d)(1)(A) gives, as a paradigm example of when a multi-term contract is appropriate, "a student bus transportation contractor would incur high start-up costs for buses and other equipment solely to meet the State's requirements but the equipment would have useful life in excess of one year."

- **Mandatory non-appropriation clause.** Every HIDOE multi-term school-bus contract is, by force of HRS section 103D-309 and HRS section 103D-315(a)/(c) and HAR section 3-122-149(b)/(c)/(g), subject to cancellation if funds are not appropriated for a succeeding fiscal period; upon such cancellation the contractor is reimbursed for reasonable nonamortized, nonrecurring costs.

- **Default source-selection method = competitive sealed bidding.** Under HRS section 103D-302(a) every new HIDOE school-bus contract award must be by competitive sealed bidding "except as otherwise provided in section 103D-301" (i.e., except via RFP, professional services, small purchase, sole source, or emergency procurement).

- **Sole-source one-year ceiling (CORRECTED CITATION).** HAR section 3-122-81(d) provides: "The contract period for a sole source procurement shall not exceed one year, unless approval is granted for a multi-term contract pursuant to section 3-122-149." (Stage-1 cited HAR 3-122-66.5; that section does not exist as a sole-source cap — HAR 3-122-66 was a distinct "Waiver to requirement for procurement of professional services" rule that has been repealed. The substantive one-year cap rule is correctly located in HAR 3-122-81(d).)

- **Short extension authority.** HAR section 3-122-3 (as amended July 10, 2025) allows the chief procurement officer to extend a contract — including one that has exhausted its built-in extension provisions — for periods of "one hundred eighty calendar days or less" each, on a written impracticability determination, party agreement, and same-or-fair-and-reasonable pricing.

- **Cooperative/piggyback purchasing.** HRS chapter 103D, Part VIII (sections 103D-801 et seq.) authorizes cooperative purchasing among public procurement units, providing an alternative to a full new IFB/RFP where applicable.

- **Emergency procurement.** HRS section 103D-307 (Emergency procurements) was effectively invoked statewide for school-bus services when the Governor's August 17, 2023 Proclamation suspended HRS chapter 103D for HIDOE school-bus driver-shortage contracting.

- **2026 session — passed but not yet act-numbered as of as-of date.** Two 2026 measures had passed both chambers and been transmitted to the Governor as of May 2026 (as of date 2026-06-23): (i) SB 2075 (RELATING TO PUBLIC PROCUREMENT) amends HRS 103D-302 and 103D-303 to add a 5% Hawaii bidder/offeror preference for evaluation purposes (capped at $500,000) and adds debarment causes; this does NOT change contract-term/rebid/renewal mechanics. (ii) HB 1785 (RELATING TO STUDENT TRANSPORTATION) adds a new HRS 302A section allowing the superintendent to temporarily suspend or remove a service segment if a student-transportation contractor fails to provide service for 5 or more consecutive instructional days and to enter an interim agreement with another qualified carrier, requiring DOE to complete "a competitive procurement under Hawaii public procurement code law for a permanent replacement service within 12 months" — confirming, not displacing, the HRS 103D framework. Neither measure has been confirmed as signed into law (Act number) by the as-of date.

- **Charter schools.** Hawaii charter schools operate under HRS chapter 302D and are STATUTORILY EXEMPT from HRS chapter 103D; they must develop internal procurement policies consistent with public-accountability goals. Their transportation procurement is not governed by HIDOE's processes. Charter schools are an explicit out-scope statutory carve-out from HIDOE-administered school-transportation contracts.

- **Special-education transportation.** HRS section 302A-406 authorizes transportation "for all children in grades kindergarten to twelve and in special education classes," but no separate Hawaii statute or HAR provision sets distinct term, rebid, renewal, or procurement rules for special-education, IEP, McKinney-Vento, foster-care, or alternative transportation. Federal IDEA/McKinney-Vento duties govern the obligation to transport but do not displace HRS chapter 103D's contracting rules.

- **No board approval requirement codified.** Nothing in HRS section 302A-407 or HRS chapter 103D requires Board of Education approval of an individual school-bus contract or its renewal; procurement decisions are made by the chief procurement officer or the head of the purchasing agency (HIDOE).

### Idaho
- **State-superintendent contract-approval gateway (Idaho Code § 33-1510(1)).** Every executed pupil transportation contract - including renewals and addenda - must be submitted to the state superintendent of public instruction *before* both parties sign for "a review of legal requirements and appropriate costs and for final approval." The superintendent has 21 calendar days from postmarked receipt to approve, recommend changes, or require changes; districts may appeal required changes to the State Board of Education, which may approve the contract without such changes.

- **Mandatory use of the SDE Model Contract (Idaho Code § 33-1510(1)).** "All contracts entered into by boards of trustees for the transportation of pupils shall be in writing using the current pupil transportation model contract developed by the state department of education." Current model version: "Model Contract for Transportation Services" available at https://www.sde.idaho.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Model-Contract-for-Transportation-Services.docx.

- **Federal Clean School Bus Program 10-year extension (Idaho Code § 33-1510(4)).** Added by 2022 ch. 87. Contracts receiving funding under the federal Clean School Bus Program (42 U.S.C. § 16091) may exceed five years up to a maximum of ten (10) years - a discrete and conditional exception to the general five-year cap.

- **Single-time renewal contingent on bid-notice disclosure.** The one-time, up-to-five-year renewal in § 33-1510(2) "shall not be granted unless the provisions of this section were included, in a substantially conforming summary, within the bidding notice, published pursuant to section 33-601, Idaho Code, of the contract." Failure to summarize the renewal authority in the original public notice forecloses the renewal - the district must then rebid.

- **All routes bid at a single time (Idaho Code § 33-1510(2)).** "School districts shall advertise, bid and contract for all bus transportation service routes at a single time, and contract with the lowest responsible bidder or bidders meeting the specifications." This prevents piecemeal route-by-route procurement.

- **Notice timing (Idaho Code § 33-402(2)).** Bidding notice must be published twice in a newspaper of general circulation, and "the notice for contracting for transportation services shall be made not less than four (4) weeks before the date of opening bids."

- **Lowest-responsible-bidder rule (Idaho Code § 33-1510(3)).** "Before entering into such contracts, the board of trustees shall invite bids by twice giving notice as provided in section 33-402(2), Idaho Code, and shall award the contract to the lowest responsible bidder."

- **CPI / fuel escalator clauses (SDE Model Contract, verbatim).** Permitted but not required: "Price escalator clauses may be included but must be proportional to an objectively determined index, such as the Consumer Price Index. Fuel escalator clauses may also be used at the discretion of the parties. However, the combination of a general price escalator term and a fuel escalator clause must be drafted carefully to protect against double compensation of the Contractor."

- **Termination for Fiscal Necessity (SDE Model Contract).** All contracts are subject to legislative appropriation, federal funding, and annual local-budget approval; the district may terminate in whole or in part within ten (10) calendar days' notice if funds are not budgeted or otherwise available. **However, the model contract expressly forecloses termination for convenience**: "Nothing in this section shall be construed as ability by the District to terminate for its convenience."

- **Termination for Cause (SDE Model Contract).** Standard default triggers - contractor insolvency, bankruptcy filing, route abandonment, refusal to proceed, schedule failure, assignment for benefit of creditors, and other defaults; the district may employ another contractor and hold the original contractor responsible for added expense or damages.

- **Title 67 ch. 28 (Purchasing by Political Subdivisions) is supplementary, not controlling.** Idaho Code § 33-601(2) directs school district contracts to be executed under Title 67 ch. 28, and § 67-2802 expressly provides: "Any general procurement procedures set forth in this chapter shall be supplemented by the provisions of any specific statute pertaining to the awarding of any contract for the purchase or acquisition of any service, commodity or thing made expressly applicable to any particular political subdivision." The transportation-specific § 33-1510 therefore controls the term cap, renewal mechanics, and bidding procedure; Title 67 ch. 28 (e.g., the $100,000/$250,000 service-procurement thresholds in § 67-2806, as amended by 2025 ch. 133, § 3) supplements as a default for matters § 33-1510 does not address.

- **No statutory small-purchase, sole-source, emergency, or piggyback/cooperative-purchasing exemption from § 33-1510 specifically for pupil transportation.** Idaho Code § 67-2803 (Title 67 ch. 28 exclusions, last amended 2025 ch. 133, § 1) lists eighteen categories exempt from general political-subdivision procurement (e.g., real-property interests, insurance, JPAs, GSA schedules, software maintenance of existing systems) - but none of these categorically exempt pupil-transportation services, and § 33-1510's transportation-specific advertising/bidding mandate is not displaced by these general exclusions. The closest related general exclusion (§ 67-2803(1)) permits a political subdivision to "duplicate the price and substance" of another competitively bid contract - practical reach for pupil transportation is uncertain and would, in any event, need to respect § 33-1510's transportation-specific bidding mandate.

- **No charter-school carve-out.** Idaho Code § 33-1510 applies to "boards of trustees" - i.e., the boards of public school districts and specially chartered school districts (see § 33-1501). Public charter schools are separately governed; their transportation contracting practices, if any, are not addressed by § 33-1510.

- **Special-education transportation is governed by the same § 33-1510 framework.** SDE Special Education guidance (REVIEWED December 2024) addresses IEP-team decisions, FAPE/LRE, behavior, and discipline - no separate procurement/term/renewal regime for special-education transportation contracts under Idaho state law.

- **No statutory cap on number of board-approved escalator adjustments within a contract term**, but escalator language must be drafted to avoid double-compensation (per SDE Model Contract guidance) and must be objectively indexed.

- **Bus-driver / vehicle-inspection / insurance requirements (§§ 33-1505 through 33-1509, 33-1515; IDAPA 08.02.02) apply to both district-operated and contractor-operated services** - these are operational standards, not procurement rules.

- **2025 ch. 278 (H 396) amendments** modernized §§ 33-1501, 33-1504, 33-1506, 33-1509, and 33-1515 (authorized-vehicle types and inspection regime) but did not amend § 33-1510. **2026 H 815** amended only § 33-1006 (Transportation Support Program funding) and did not touch § 33-1510. § 33-1510 has not been amended since 2022 ch. 87.

### Illinois
- **Small-purchase / dollar-threshold exemptions (105 ILCS 5/10-20.21(a)).** The $35,000 sealed-bid/public-advertisement threshold (or a lower amount fixed by board policy) applies to school-district contracts generally, but the pupil-transportation carve-out at 10-20.21(a)(xvi) substitutes a sealed-RFP framework keyed to safety/comfort/stability/quality first and price second regardless of dollar amount once the district elects to contract for service. - **Cooperative purchasing / piggyback authority.**
  - **Governmental Joint Purchasing Act (30 ILCS 525/).** School districts (as taxing public entities / governmental units) are eligible to make joint purchases of supplies and services, including transportation services, with one or more other governmental units, subject to competitive solicitation conducted by one of the participating units; the Act expressly supersedes inconsistent procurement provisions when joint authority is exercised. A district may "review and consider as a bid under subsection (a) of this Section certified education purchasing contracts that are already available through the State education purchasing entity." (Source: https://www.ilga.gov/documents/legislation/ilcs/documents/010500050K10-20.21.htm.)
  - **District-to-district contracts (105 ILCS 5/29-6).** "Any school district, including any non-high school district, may contract at actual cost with 1 or more school districts for the transportation of pupils to and from the school attended" (P.A. 78-1245) — a recognized alternative to procuring from a private contractor.
- **Mandatory rebid trigger when district self-operates (105 ILCS 5/29-6.4).** A board operating its own bus fleet must solicit sealed bids on receipt of a "timely" certified-mail request from a private contractor; the board may then either contract with the lowest responsible bidder pursuant to 29-6.1 or continue self-operating after publicly disclosing the bid amounts and the district's fully allocated costs at a regular board meeting within 30 days. - **Charter schools.** Illinois charter schools operate under Article 27A of the School Code (105 ILCS 5/27A-1 et seq.) and their charters; charter schools generally are not subject to the School Code procurement and transportation-contracting provisions in the same manner as traditional districts unless their charter so provides. - **Special-education differences (operational, not statutory).** No different state-law term/rebid/renewal rule applies. 29-6.4 expressly references "special education cooperatives" alongside districts, confirming uniform application of the rebid trigger. (See specialEducation field.)
- **State-approval requirements.** No state-agency pre-approval is required for a district to enter into, renew, or extend a pupil-transportation contract within the 29-6.1 framework. ISBE approval is required for transportation-reimbursement claims (105 ILCS 5/29-5 and 23 Ill. Adm. Code Part 120 series), not for contract formation.
- **Cross-cutting contract terms required or recognized by 29-6.1.** (a) "Termination option after 5 years" is mandatory for non-EV contracts longer than 5 years. (b) "Contract opener clauses for any purpose" are expressly permitted (e.g., fuel, CPI, regulatory-change openers). (c) EV-utilizing contracts may go to 15 years only if "the contract relies on capital or infrastructure purchases or improvements that cannot reasonably be justified in a shorter-term contract."
- **Anti-challenge shield (105 ILCS 5/10-20.21(a)).** "At no time shall a cause of action lie against a school board for awarding a pupil transportation contract per the standards set forth in this subsection (a) unless the cause of action is based on fraudulent conduct." This sharply limits losing-bidder protests of pupil-transportation award decisions.
- **Stale ISBE guidance.** ISBE's published "Pupil Transportation FAQs" answer to the contract-length FAQ still recites the pre-2024 statutory text. The statute controls; the FAQ is not authority and reads the prior version of 29-6.1. (Identified during adversarial review and noted in District Variance.)
- **Driver-permit / contractor-conduct overlay (625 ILCS 5/6-106.11).** A contracted operator must employ drivers holding a valid School Bus Driver Permit, supply driver names and permit copies to the district, notify the district within 2 business days of any permit suspension or cancellation, refrain from using salvage/junk-status vehicles, and prohibit smoking in contract vehicles. Violations are a business offense (first offense fine $1,000–$10,000) with escalating penalties on repeat violation.

### Indiana
- **Procurement framework is IC 5-22, not IC 20-27.** IC 20-27-5-7 explicitly requires that "Transportation and fleet contracts entered into by a school corporation shall be entered into under IC 5-22." The school-transportation chapter's own former bidding-and-specifications provisions (IC 20-27-5-8 through 9, 11 through 12, 14 through 18, 20, 22, 24 through 25) are now Repealed in the current Indiana Code (verified verbatim against DOE 2025 booklet).
- **Services vs. supplies distinction is decisive.** IC 5-22-17-3 caps SUPPLIES contracts at 4 years (5 for county/municipal-hospital purchases under IC 5-22-7). Student transportation is a SERVICE, not a supply, and falls under IC 5-22-6-1's "any procedure the governmental body … considers appropriate" rule. The 4-year cap does not apply to a transportation services contract on its statutory face.
- **Small-purchase thresholds (general IC 5-22 services framework).** SBOA-published guidance treats IC 5-22 small-purchase thresholds as: below $50,000, small-purchase policies under IC 5-22-8-2; $50,000-$150,000, quotes from at least three vendors; above $150,000, IC 5-22-7 sealed bidding for supplies. These thresholds chiefly drive supplies-procurement; the IC 5-22-6 services rule lets the local body define its own services procedure. (Source: AIM Indiana Public Purchasing Guide and SBOA IASBO Purchasing presentation.)
- **Sole-source / special purchasing methods.** IC 5-22-10 authorizes specific exceptions (single source for supplies — IC 5-22-10-13, emergency, etc.). These are written-determination based and apply chiefly to supplies; their applicability to transportation services is mediated through IC 5-22-6's "any procedure" rule.
- **Cooperative purchasing / piggyback.** A school corporation may enter cooperative-purchasing arrangements under the Interlocal Cooperation Act (IC 36-1-7) and IC 5-22. The Indiana Department of Administration's K12Indiana.com portal provides statewide purchase agreements accessible to school corporations and charter schools.
- **Notice requirement (transportation-specific).** IC 20-27-5-10(a) requires the governing body to give public notice "at least ten (10) days before beginning negotiations or receiving proposals or bids for transportation or fleet contracts" (notice given in the manner of IC 5-3-1); (b) prohibits negotiating a transportation/fleet contract without this notice. This applies to new awards and re-solicitations.
- **Bidder prerequisites.** IC 20-27-5-13 conditions any transportation contract award on the bidder (i) meeting IC 20-27-8-1 physical requirements per a certificate from an Indiana physician, and (ii) holding a valid public passenger chauffeur's license or CDL.
- **Surety bond.** IC 20-27-5-28 requires the contractor to "furnish a surety bond conditioned on faithful performance of the contract"; the governing body sets the amount.
- **Sale or assignment.** IC 20-27-5-19 — "A transportation or fleet contract entered into under this chapter may not be sold or assigned except by written agreement of both parties to the original contract and by the assignee or purchaser of the contract."
- **Equipment buyout on cancellation.** IC 20-27-5-27 — on cancellation, the governing body may purchase the contractor's school-bus equipment at "fair market value" determined by agreement of the parties.
- **Termination for alcohol violation (no hearing).** IC 20-27-5-23 — a transportation contract may be terminated without hearing on reliable evidence of alcohol consumption by the driver during school hours, while operating a school bus, or while performing the school bus driver's duties.
- **Non-appropriation cancellation (IC 5-22-17-5).** Written determination by the fiscal body that funds are not appropriated/available cancels the contract; this determination is "final and conclusive."
- **Charter schools.** Charter schools (IC 20-24) are included within "school corporation" for several enumerated purposes; charter procurement is governed by Indiana Charter School Board procurement policies and the IC 5-22 framework generally. Charter Operations-Fund usage for transportation is governed by IC 20-40-18-10.5.
- **TNC option.** IC 20-27-5-5(c) and 5-6(c) authorize school corporations to enter "transportation agreement[s]" or "fleet agreement[s]" with a Transportation Network Company (TNC, IC 8-2.1-17-18) if expanded criminal-history and CPS-index checks per IC 20-26-5-10 are performed on every TNC driver (IC 8-2.1-17-19) who will transport students.
- **Common-carrier contracts.** IC 20-27-5-29 through 5-32 allow contracts with regular-route common carriers under DOR jurisdiction with their own specifications regime (carrier solely responsible for driver employment/conduct; driver physical certificate required; exempt from certain construction/equipment specs subject to operational conditions).
- **Special-education transportation.** No separate state-law contracting rule (IC 20-35-8-2 governs IEP transportation extent and cost allocation, not contracting term/rebid/renewal).
- **Homeless-student transportation.** No separate contracting rule (IC 20-27-12-4 inter-corporation cost-sharing; IC 20-27-12-5 vehicle types).
- **Foster-care transportation.** No separate contracting rule (IC 20-50-3 cost allocation; IC 20-27-12-5 vehicle types).
- **Township-trustee carve-out (historical residue).** Secondary indexed sources reference a rule that "a township trustee may not enter into a school bus contract that has a term extending beyond the June 30 following the expiration date of the trustee's term of office." Adversarial review: this rule is NOT visible in the current IC 20-27-5 text in the DOE 2025 booklet — direct PDF extraction confirms it does not appear in sections 1 through 33 of the current chapter. It appears to be residual from the pre-2005 IC 20-9.1-2 framework and applied to a township-trustee-as-school-authority structure that no longer exists in current Indiana school governance. Treated as historical/inapplicable; not a generally applicable rule on private contractors today.
- **Renewal cap (IC 5-22-17-4(c)).** Each renewal may not exceed the original term, but unlimited renewals are permitted with mutual agreement — this gives Indiana a de facto "term + unlimited equal-or-shorter renewals" structure rather than a hard ceiling.
- **General governing-body authority.** IC 20-26-5-4(a)(10) — "Subject to IC 20-27-13, to transport children to and from school, when in the opinion of the governing body the transportation is necessary, including considerations for the safety of the children" — is the umbrella discretion grant under which a board contracts (or self-operates).

### Iowa
- **Mandatory DOE-prescribed uniform contract.** Iowa Code § 285.5(5) commands: "The director of the department of education shall prepare a uniform contract containing provisions not in conflict with this chapter which shall be used by all schools in contracting for transportation service." The current form is IDOE Form TR-F-4-497 (Rev. 12/03), "CONTRACT for PUBLIC STUDENT TRANSPORTATION with a Contractor" (https://educate.iowa.gov/media/2087/download?inline). The contract form's term line carries a bracketed limit "[Period not to exceed 3 years.]" Districts may add "Additional Provisions" but cannot displace the chapter-285 requirements.
- **Seventeen mandatory contract content provisions (IAC 281—43.21).** The current Iowa Administrative Code (adopted by ARC 7793C effective 5/22/24) lists 17 provisions every § 285.5 contract must contain, including: contractor furnishes/operates a legally approved vehicle (43.21(1)); compliance with uniform standards (43.21(2)–(4)); mechanical condition and inspection (43.21(5)–(6)); board-approved drivers only with current school-bus-driver authorization (43.21(7)); medical exam certificate (43.21(8)); driver in-service instruction (43.21(9)); insurance with policy on file with superintendent (43.21(10)); reports to DOE/AEA/superintendent (43.21(11)); restricted use to enrolled students and approved extracurriculars (43.21(12)); reporting of bus-passing-law violations (43.21(13)); board's right to change routing of the bus with extra-mileage allowance (43.21(14)); sanctions for immoral conduct or alcohol use (43.21(15)); the **90-day mutual termination clause** per § 285.5(4) (43.21(16)); and the contractor-to-board **equipment buy-out** on contractor-initiated termination per § 285.5(1) (43.21(17), with an express exception for passenger vehicles used as school buses).
- **No general state-mandated competitive-bidding threshold for school district services.** Iowa Code ch. 26 ("Public Construction Bidding") and § 73A.18 apply only to the "construction, erection, demolition, alteration or repair of a public improvement" (verbatim from § 73A.18), not to operating service contracts. School districts set their own thresholds at which they will solicit competitive quotations or bids for services such as transportation; representative templates (Iowa School Finance Information Services 705.1 / 705.01, used by districts including Iowa City CSD and South Tama County CSD) reflect this discretion. Although nothing requires a rebid at the end of a § 285.5 term, districts commonly run RFPs at term-end as a matter of board policy and good practice, not state-law command.
- **Equipment buy-out on contractor termination.** Iowa Code § 285.5(1)(b): if the contractor desires to terminate the contract and the board desires to purchase the equipment, the contractor must sell at a price set by a three-member appraisal board (one chosen by the board, one by the contractor, third selected by those two). IAC 281—43.21(17) repeats this requirement but expressly exempts passenger vehicles used as school buses.
- **90-day termination right is absolute and mutual.** § 285.5(4) makes the termination right available to either party; IAC 281—43.21(16) makes it a mandatory contract clause; the IDOE form clarifies that termination may not be effective mid-semester absent both parties' agreement.
- **Mandatory contractor-outside-board rule.** § 285.5(7) requires transportation service contracts and driver contracts for school-owned buses to be "made with someone outside the board" except where no other service is available (in which case a board member may transport their own children).
- **Insurance, vehicle, and driver requirements not optional.** § 285.5(6) requires liability insurance "in amounts and kind as provided in the official contract"; § 285.5(3) requires every contractor vehicle to be inspected, approved, and certified before operation; IAC 281—43.7 requires contractor vehicles to meet school-bus construction/equipment standards; IAC 281—43.21(7) requires every contractor driver to hold a current school-bus-driver authorization.
- **Cooperative purchasing / piggybacking.** Iowa Code ch. 28E ("Joint Exercise of Governmental Powers") authorizes interlocal agreements among public agencies, including school districts and AEAs. Districts may cooperate to procure transportation services or to share an AEA-operated arrangement for special-education transportation under § 256B.4(1)(e). The § 285.5 form and three-year cap still govern any private-contractor leg of such an arrangement.
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement.** Iowa Code ch. 285 and IAC 281—ch. 43 contain no sole-source or emergency-procurement provisions specific to transportation. Because there is no state-mandated competitive bidding for transportation services, no formal "sole-source exemption" is needed; a board may award without competition unless its own policy requires otherwise.
- **Charter school rules.** Iowa Code ch. 256E governs charter schools; transportation contracting by charters is generally addressed in the charter contract.
- **Common-carrier transportation.** Iowa Code § 285.1(5) allows districts to use common carriers under uniform DOE standards when school-bus transportation is impracticable; this is a separate authorization track, not a § 285.5 contract.
- **Pricing limits for nonpublic-pupil transportation contracts.** Iowa Code § 285.1(17)(b) provides that when a district contracts with a private party to satisfy nonpublic-pupil transportation duties under § 285.5, the contract "shall not provide payment in excess of the average per pupil transportation costs of the school district for that year." This is a price ceiling, not a procurement-method requirement.
- **No statutory CPI/index, fuel escalator, or evergreen renewal mechanism.** Such mechanisms, if used, must be written into the negotiated terms of a § 285.5 contract and re-executed (within the 3-year cap) at term-end as a new contract.

### Kansas
- **Sealed-bid threshold ($20,000) applies only to construction/goods/materials, not services.** K.S.A. 72-1151(a) requires sealed bidding for school-district expenditures over $20,000 only for 'construction, reconstruction or remodeling or for the purchase of materials, goods or wares.' Subsection (b)(1) excludes 'Services' from that requirement; subsection (b)(4) separately excludes 'motor fuels required to provide or furnish transportation.' Last amended L. 2003, ch. 53, § 2.
- **Interpretive tension on the breadth of (b)(1) 'Services'.** AG Op. 1993-008 narrowly construed the (b)(1) exemption to reach only 'professional services, such as architectural, engineering, medical, financial, consulting, and other similar services' and held dirt-hauling subject to mandatory bidding. AG Op. 2008-009 quoted (b)(1) as the bare word 'Services' when distinguishing pure construction-management services (exempt) from construction-manager-at-risk agreements (subject to bidding). The current statutory text is the bare word 'Services' with no qualifier; Kansas school-district practice has uniformly applied the exemption to private student-transportation services contracts. The tension has not been definitively resolved by a published Kansas appellate decision specifically addressing student-transportation contracts.
- **In-district local-bidder preference.** K.S.A. 72-1151(c) creates a 1% domiciliary-bidder preference: a school-district-domiciled bidder whose bid is within 1% of the lowest responsible bid may match the low bid within 72 hours and be awarded the contract. Subsection (d) makes this preference inapplicable to construction, reconstruction, or remodeling.
- **Transportation-specific contracting authority.** K.S.A. 72-6498 (formerly K.S.A. 72-8316) lets a board contract with townships, cities, counties, nonpublic schools, community colleges, public recreation commissions, other school districts (cooperative shared-cost basis), and four-year colleges/universities/area vocational schools for transportation when buses are not needed for regular school purposes. Revenues are general-fund reimbursements.
- **Parent-driver mileage contracts.** K.S.A. 72-6489 (formerly K.S.A. 72-8304) authorizes a board to contract with a parent/driver to transport a student to the prescribed school route or school building when the residence cannot practically be reached or scheduling a bus is impracticable. Mileage rate is capped at the K.S.A. 75-3203 state mileage rate; limited to two round trips per day; only one student's mileage paid where multiple students share a vehicle.
- **Interschool-district transportation contracts.** K.S.A. 72-6492 (formerly K.S.A. 72-8307) allows a board to contract with any other board of education for transportation of students, consistent with K.S.A. 72-6487.
- **Interlocal cooperation.** K.S.A. 12-2901 et seq. (Interlocal Cooperation Act) and the school-district interlocal cooperation statutes allow two or more public agencies (including school districts) to jointly procure transportation services. Most interlocal agreements must be approved by the Kansas Attorney General.
- **Cooperative / cooperative purchasing.** The Kansas Bus Purchasing Program (administered through KSDE/Department of Administration channels) is a voluntary program letting USDs and interlocal cooperatives buy school buses at state bid pricing through the Kansas eSupplier Public Portal. K.S.A. 72-1151(b)(2) and the cross-references to K.S.A. 75-3317 et seq. support cooperative purchasing of goods.
- **Lease/lease-purchase term cap (10 years), with bus-lease exclusion.** K.S.A. 72-1149 lets a USD enter a lease or lease-purchase of real or personal property 'not to exceed 10 years,' and exempts these from the cash-basis law. The board may NOT lease school buses as lessor under this authority.
- **Cash-basis law.** K.S.A. 10-1112/10-1113 prohibit a school district (a 'municipality' under K.S.A. 10-1101, which lists 'school district' in the definition) from creating indebtedness in excess of funds on hand. K.S.A. 10-1113 specifically allows cancelable purchase orders for school buses, supplies, books, and data processing equipment in advance of the budget year, but bars purchase contracts entered before the budget year of payment. Multi-year services contracts are typically structured with annual non-appropriation clauses to comply.
- **Construction-only alternative project delivery and bidding.** K.S.A. 72-1154 (and 72-1148/72-1150 ancillary provisions) govern construction project delivery and bidding — not transportation services.
- **State Use Program / state-agency procurement does not apply.** K.S.A. 75-3739 (state-agency competitive procurement) reaches 'state agencies' as defined in Chapter 75, Article 37, not unified school districts. School districts are governed by Chapter 72, Article 11 (72-1151 et seq.).
- **Federal duty-to-transport overlay (special education / McKinney-Vento).** IDEA related services (34 C.F.R. § 300.34(c)(16)) and the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act (42 U.S.C. § 11432) require districts to provide transportation in defined circumstances. These are duties on the district side and do not modify the state-law procurement rules governing the contractor side.
- **Charter/innovative-school transportation.** Kansas does not have a separate authorizer-charter regime altering pupil-transportation procurement; 'innovative districts' and contract schools operate under the same Chapter 72 procurement backdrop.
- **DA-146a contract form.** Any USD contract with a state agency vendor commonly incorporates Department of Administration Form DA-146a mandatory contract provisions; this is a contract-form requirement, not a transportation-specific rule.
- **Practical implication.** Because state law neither caps the term nor unambiguously mandates rebidding for private bus-services contracts, contract terms in Kansas typically range from 3 to 7 years (with renewal/extension provisions, CPI/fuel escalators, and annual-appropriation language as a function of contract drafting and the K.S.A. 10-1112/10-1113 backdrop). Shawnee Mission USD 512 (7-year, Jan. 2026, Zum), Olathe USD 233 (5-year, 2015, First Student renewal), and Andover USD 385 (Mar. 2023, First Student) reflect this range as a matter of district practice, not state-law mandate.

### Kentucky
- **Dual procurement-code election (KRS 45A.343).** Every Kentucky school district must either (a) elect to adopt the Kentucky Model Procurement Code at KRS 45A.345–45A.460 by board action, in which case no other purchasing statute applies, or (b) operate under the default school-district Bid Law at KRS 424.260 with implementing regulation 702 KAR 3:135. The choice materially affects what process exceptions are available, but neither path imposes a contract-term cap on pupil-transportation contracts.
- **Small-purchase threshold ($40,000) — same under both regimes.** KRS 45A.385 (KMPC) and KRS 424.260(1) (default bid law) each set $40,000 as the dollar threshold below which simpler small-purchase procedures may be used. Both statutes were last amended in the 2023 Regular Session, effective June 29, 2023 (Acts ch. 152, sec. 1 and ch. 152, sec. 2 respectively). Because a typical route or full-service pupil-transportation contract will exceed $40,000, the small-purchase exception will rarely apply to a full bus contract — but it can apply to ancillary services.
- **Noncompetitive-negotiation grounds (KRS 45A.380).** A district that has adopted the KMPC may contract noncompetitively only on a written determination of: emergency, single source within a reasonable geographic area, licensed professional/technician/artist services (specifically excluding construction-management services from architects and engineers), perishable foods, replacement parts where need cannot reasonably be anticipated, proprietary items for resale, student-operated enterprise, expenditures on authorized out-of-boundary trips, supplies sold at public auction, group insurance, supplies at reduced prices producing savings, or certain private-developer sewer/stormwater agreements. "Single source" or "emergency" are the only ones plausibly applicable to a transportation contract.
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority (KRS 45A.420; KRS 424.260(3); KRS 156.076).** A local public agency may enter cooperative-purchasing agreements with other local public agencies and rely on the contracting agency's compliance with KRS 45A.365/.370/.375/.380. School districts also may purchase under state, GSA, or other-district contracts on a "price reduction" basis. KSBA Model Policy 04.32 (Adopted/Amended 8/26/2025) authorizes piggybacking on contracts of the State Division of Purchases, federal (GSA), cooperative agencies, or another school district's bid. In practice, districts frequently use cooperative bus-purchasing arrangements (e.g., KISTA bus-finance program). Cooperative purchasing reduces or eliminates the need for a fresh competitive procurement at expiration if a current cooperative contract covers the need.
- **Insurance and bonding (702 KAR 5:030 §§ 11, 22, 23; KRS 160.310; KRS 45A.435).** A district must require annual performance bonds from contracted bus operators; must secure liability/indemnity insurance for contracted vehicles at department-set minimums; and, if a construction component triggers KRS 45A.435 (>$100,000 construction contracts), must require performance and payment bonds at 100% of contract value. These are bonding requirements, not term requirements, but they constrain how a multi-year contract is documented (e.g., bond renewal each year).
- **Disclosure / compliance requirements (KRS 45A.343(2)–(4) and KRS 45A.485).** Every contract entered after July 15, 1994 by a local public agency — under KMPC or any other authority — must require the contractor and all subcontractors to reveal any final determination of a violation within the preceding five-year period under KRS Chapters 136, 139, 141, 337, 338, 341, and 342, and to remain in continuous compliance with those chapters for the duration of the contract; failure is grounds for cancellation and two-year disqualification of the contractor (and disqualification of any subcontractor). Drug-testing, background-check, and licensure requirements apply to contractor-employed drivers under KRS 156.153 (as amended by HB 447), KRS 156.990 (penalty for unlicensed transportation), and KRS 160.380 (background checks; also amended by HB 447).
- **Alternative student transportation (KRS 156.153(3); 2024 Ky. Acts ch. 35 [HB 447]; 2025 Ky. Acts ch. 151).** Beginning April 4, 2024 (further amended effective April 1, 2025), a district may transport students in district-owned, leased, OR contracted passenger vehicles for nine (9) or fewer occupants under an alternative-transportation plan approved by the Kentucky Department of Education, subject to administrative regulations to be promulgated by the Kentucky Board of Education on minimum vehicle standards, minimum insurance, route safety, pick-up/drop-off protocols, and driver qualifications/training/drug-testing (49 C.F.R. pt. 40). The statute does not specify a contract-term cap for contracted alternative-transportation vehicles.
- **Charter schools (KRS 160.1591 et seq.).** Public charter schools are subject to the charter contract framework in KRS Chapter 160; the screened authoritative materials do not document any charter-school-specific term/rebid/renewal rule for pupil-transportation procurement that differs from the framework that applies to traditional public school districts.
- **State-approval requirements / KDE reimbursement.** Transportation cost reimbursement under KRS 157.360 and 702 KAR 5:020 (program cost calculation) is calculated using the state's transportation funding formula; the formula and SEEK-cost-of-transportation reimbursement are administered by KDE's Office of Finance and Operations and do not impose any direct contractual term/rebid rule.
- **Federal Uniform Guidance overlay (2 C.F.R. Part 200).** Contracts paid in whole or in part with federal funds (e.g., IDEA, Title I, McKinney-Vento, ESSER) are independently subject to the procurement standards at 2 C.F.R. §§ 200.318–200.327, including the conflict-of-interest, full-and-open-competition, and contract-administration requirements; the KSBA model policy 04.32 incorporates the Part 200 conflict-of-interest standard. Federal Uniform Guidance does not impose a per-state contract-term cap.
- **702 KAR 5:030 § 10 annual-instrument framework.** This is the closest thing to a "term" rule in Kentucky and is regulatory rather than statutory. A district must issue a written contract each school year clearly establishing contractor responsibilities, and an annual performance bond. This is consistent with — and contemplated to coexist with — multi-year contractual relationships under KSBA model § 3.26, where the multi-year contract is the master agreement and an annual written contract / bond instrument is issued under it each year.

### Louisiana
- Owner-operator system as the historical default. Louisiana's distinctive feature is that "school bus operator" is statutorily defined as "any employee of any city, parish, or other local public school board whose duty it is to transport students in any board school bus" (R.S. 17:491), even when the operator owns the bus. The dominant historical model is an individual driver who personally operates and (often) owns the bus, with tenure protection for pre-July 1, 2012 hires (R.S. 17:492; LAC 28:CXIII §309). Personal-operation requirement recurs across district policy manuals citing Bulletin 119.

- Tenure abolished prospectively. La. R.S. 17:492 (and LAC 28:CXIII §309(B)): "School bus operators starting employment with a school system on July 1, 2012, or thereafter are not eligible for tenure and may be removed from their position as provided by the personnel policy of the employing school board."

- Filling a vacant route — statutory sequence (R.S. 17:493.1; LAC 28:CXIII §1505): (1) most-senior tenured operator offered first, in seniority order; (2) full-time probationary operator; (3) substitute operator from approved list; (4) special owner-retirement provision allowing a buyer of the retiring operator's bus (at appraised value) to receive the route, where the bus is no more than 5 model-years old and the retirement is for documented physical disability. This is the closest Louisiana analogue to a "rebid at term-end" and is non-competitive.

- Frozen-mileage protection (R.S. 17:497; LAC 28:CXIII §1703(B)): "Frozen mileage guarantees that the contract owner/operator cannot be penalized by a reduction of compensated mileage … for a period of seven years when a new bus is purchased or five years when a used bus not more than five model year old is purchased." Operational structural effect: a parish that contracts with individual owner-operators effectively commits to a multi-year compensation floor.

- Court-ordered route change protection (R.S. 17:497.1) — CORRECTED: applies in St. Landry Parish only. The statute states: "the provisions of this section shall apply in St. Landry Parish only." It is NOT a statewide protection (Stage 1 wording corrected here).

- Services contracts outside the Public Bid Law. LLA Public Bid Law FAQ (Revised 02/2026), A.87-88: services — including transportation — are not subject to the Public Bid Law for local political subdivisions; competitive procurement is therefore discretionary, not mandatory. LDOE Office of School Choice FAQ confirms only public-works contracts trigger mandatory bidding for school boards. LASBO Workshop 11 states verbatim: "Public Bid Law does not apply to the purchase of Services — Professional or Otherwise."

- Materials/supplies thresholds (background, not a transportation-contract rule). For materials and supplies, parishes must competitively bid contracts at or over $60,000 effective February 1, 2026 (LLA FAQ A.1 / R.S. 38:2212(C)(1)); written/electronic quotes apply between $30,000 and $60,000. These thresholds reach a bus purchase but not a transportation services contract.

- Bus-purchase pooling and sale-to-operator authority. R.S. 17:158.2 and 17:158.3 authorize LEAs to purchase buses and resell to operators, and to pool bus purchases. R.S. 17:158.7 and Bulletin 119 §2507 authorize leases between LEAs and operators or authorized businesses.

- Fleet/group insurance contracts. LAC 28:CXIII §2513(D), verbatim: "Insurance for Contracted Services. State law authorizes LEAs contracting for the use of privately owned school buses to procure contracts on a fleet or group basis for the owners who are insuring the vehicles." This is an insurance-procurement authorization, not a competitive-procurement requirement for the underlying transportation services.

- Charter schools. La. R.S. 17:3996 makes charters exempt from most public-school statutes but lists exceptions. BESE-authorized charters that provide transportation must submit a Transportation Plan to LDOE annually by July 31 and abide by R.S. 17:158 and the approved plan (BESE Authorized Charter School Transportation Plan). Note: the LDOE charter plan document references R.S. 17:158 generally; older references to "R.S. 17:158(J)" in some materials reflect prior subsection lettering that has shifted following Acts 2023, No. 362 — current 17:158(J) addresses loading/unloading procedures.

- Cooperative purchasing / "piggyback." Available under the Public Bid Law (R.S. 38:2212.1) and the Louisiana Procurement Code (R.S. 39:1701 et seq.) for materials and supplies that bear on transportation (e.g., bus purchases) — but not the vehicle of choice for services contracts, which are unregulated for competitive procurement at the local-board level.

- Sole-source / emergency / small-purchase exemptions. LLA Public Bid Law FAQ details emergency, extreme-emergency, and other Public Bid Law exceptions for materials/supplies and public works; because transportation services are already outside the Public Bid Law, these exceptions are not directly relevant to a transportation-services contract.

- State approval. No general BESE/LDOE pre-approval required to enter a private transportation contract; LDOE School Bus Transportation Program provides training and technical assistance to transportation supervisors and private contractors, but this is technical-assistance, not pre-clearance.

- Discontinuation of service. R.S. 17:158(H) requires State Board approval before a parish eliminates transportation services. This is a service-discontinuation control, not a contract-renewal control.

- Recent legislative activity. R.S. 17:158 was most recently amended by Acts 2023, No. 362, §1, per the official Louisiana Legislature page (re-verified 2026-06-23). 2024 SB 416 was identified but did not result in a substantive amendment affecting contract term/rebid/renewal rules for private transportation contracts. The statute as currently published has subsections A through K; none cap term, mandate bidding, or set renewal mechanics for private contracts.

### Maine
- **Small-purchase exemption from sealed bidding.** 20-A M.R.S. §5402(5)(A) exempts "Contracts for bus services under $4,000" from the sealed-bid procedure. The $4,000 threshold has not been updated since enactment in PL 1983, c. 175 and is rarely operative for full-route service contracts.
- **Inter-school-unit exemption.** 20-A M.R.S. §5402(5)(B) exempts "Contracts between school administration units" from the sealed-bid requirement, providing the only statutory authority for SAU-to-SAU shared-transportation arrangements without bidding. Combined with §5401(2)(B) (SAD secondary-school-privileges authority) and §5401(3)(B) (community school district committees' written-agreement authority), this creates a limited inter-governmental alternative to private competitive procurement.
- **Sole-source, emergency, cooperative-purchasing, piggyback authority — not provided for private-contractor service contracts.** The transportation statute contains no sole-source, emergency-procurement, cooperative-purchasing, interlocal/piggyback, or board-waiver authority specific to SAU-to-private-contractor pupil-transportation contracts. State-level cooperative purchasing under Title 5 does not extend to SAU transportation contracts (Title 5 §1825-B reaches only state agencies).
- **Education service centers (Chapter 123) — alternative non-private-contractor pathway.** 20-A M.R.S. Chapter 123 authorizes SAU members of an Education Service Center to purchase services from the center, including "Transportation, transportation routing and vehicle maintenance" as a Category 4 facilities-and-transportation service. This is an inter-governmental services pathway (SAU member purchasing from an Education Service Center), not a substitute for the §5402 sealed-bid requirement when an SAU contracts with a private contractor.
- **State-board cooperative-recommendation framework (§405).** 20-A M.R.S. §405 authorizes the state board to recommend that SAUs develop cooperative agreements that "may include, but are not limited to: purchasing or contract agreements," but this is a state-board recommendation framework — not direct statutory authority to bypass §5402 for transportation services from a private contractor.
- **Commissioner's role.** The commissioner's authority over private-contractor transportation contracts is limited to (i) authorizing the one-year extension under §5401(13)(A) and (ii) approving school-bus purchases, contracts and leases (i.e., the bus-acquisition side) under §5401(15) (separate $5,000,000 annual cap on purchase approvals and 5-year maximum loan/lease-purchase term, extended to 15 years for zero-emission buses by PL 2021, c. 693, §2). The commissioner does not pre-approve every transportation service contract; under 05-071 C.M.R. Chapter 81 §8, SAUs must present the contract for DOE review "upon request" along with a §5402 compliance statement.
- **Governing-body authorization.** §5401(13)(B) — any transportation contract "existing beyond one year" requires authorization by the SAU's governing body. In a SAD, §5401(13)(C) requires the superintendent to execute the contract with the approval of the board of directors. These are concurrent requirements: a multi-year contract requires both the §5402 sealed-bid process and §5401(13)(B)/(C) board/governing-body authorization.
- **Award standard.** §5402 prescribes sealed-bid mechanics but does not codify a "lowest responsible bidder" award standard. School law commentary (e.g., Drummond Woodsum School Law Advisory 267, citing 20-A M.R.S. §5401(13)(D) and §5402) describes the customary practice as award to the lowest responsible bidder with discretion to reject non-conforming bids, but this is practice/guidance rather than a statutory mandate.
- **Bus acquisition vs. service contracts.** The 5-year ceiling in §5401(13)(A) governs service contracts; a parallel 5-year ceiling applies to loans and lease-purchase agreements for bus acquisition under §5401(15)(C), extended to 15 years for zero-emission school buses (PL 2021, c. 693, §2).
- **Special-education transportation.** No separate procurement framework; general §5401(13)/§5402 controls. §7204(1) treats transportation as a "related service" the commissioner must cause to be provided; §7251-A permits SAUs to contract for "special education services" and "related services." See specialEducation field.
- **Charter schools — exempt from SAU statutes including §5401/§5402.** Title 20-A §2412 provides: "Except as provided in this chapter and its charter contract, a public charter school is exempt from all statutes and rules applicable to a noncharter public school, a local school board or a school administrative unit." Charter schools must "have a plan that describes how the school will provide transportation" but are not bound by the §5401(13) term cap or the §5402 sealed-bid procedure for their own transportation procurement. Civil rights, health/safety, assessment, conflict-of-interest, public records, criminal-history-checks, and special-education requirements remain non-exemptable.
- **DOE administrative rule.** 05-071 C.M.R. Chapter 81 §8 ("Contracts for School Transportation Services") requires SAUs to present transportation/lease contracts to DOE for review upon request along with a §5402 compliance statement; statutory authority is 29-A M.R.S. §2311 and 20-A M.R.S. §5401(17); repealed and replaced effective September 16, 2017.
- **State-level Maine School Bus Bid Module.** Maine DOE / Division of Procurement Services jointly operate a statewide Invitation-to-Bid module for school bus *vehicle purchases* (https://www.maine.gov/doe/schools/transportation/programs/busbidmodule) — this is a vehicle-procurement vehicle, not a service-contract vehicle, and does not displace §5402 for SAU-to-private-contractor service contracts.
- **General municipal/SAD contracting statutes.** Title 30-A M.R.S. §124 (municipal bids/awards) and Title 20-A §1314 (SAD bid procedure) are parallel provisions but the controlling, transportation-specific statute is §5402, expressly cross-referenced from §5401(13)(D). §1314(5) (Exceptions) is REPEALED — confirmed on 2026-06-23 via the official §1314 page. Title 5 M.R.S. §1825-B (state procurement) applies only to state agencies and does not reach SAUs.

### Maryland
- **Statutory threshold for competitive bidding (Educ. § 5-112(b)).** A county board must competitively bid contracts for which 'the cost is more than $100,000' (or the amount in State Finance & Procurement Art. § 13-109(a), whichever applies). Advertisement of at least two weeks before bid opening is required in a medium accessible to the general public (newspaper of general circulation, eMaryland Marketplace, or the local school system's website). Specifications must give a clear functional description and (unless a specified manufacturer is required for compatibility, student health, replacement-parts/maintenance compatibility, or resale) must allow equivalent products.
- **Award standard (Educ. § 5-112(c)).** Contracts go to the responsible bidder who provides the 'best value and conforms to specifications,' weighing quantity, delivery time, purpose, bidder competency, ability to service, minority-contractor utilization plans, and price. The board may reject all bids and re-advertise.
- **Void clause (Educ. § 5-112(i)).** 'A contract entered into or purchase made in violation of this section is void.'
- **Small-purchase / threshold exemption (Educ. § 5-112).** Contracts at or below the § 5-112(b) threshold are not subject to the formal advertising requirement; each board's own purchasing policy controls (e.g., Charles County: <$2,500 and $2,500-$25,000 tiers; Cecil County: competitive quotations for purchases at or above $25,000 but under $50,000).
- **Emergency procurement (Educ. § 5-112(a)(2)).** Express exemption from competitive bidding for 'emergency repairs' — useful in pupil-transportation contexts (e.g., emergency replacement of routes after contractor failure mid-year, the express authority cited in St. Mary's non-public procedures, Paragraph 12).
- **Cooperative purchasing / piggyback (Educ. § 5-112(a)(3); State Fin. & Proc. § 13-110).** A county board may participate in contracts for goods or commodities awarded by other public agencies or by intergovernmental purchasing organizations IF the lead agency followed public-bidding procedures. This is the statutory pathway by which a board may avoid running its own pupil-transportation RFP when a usable lead-agency competitively bid contract exists.
- **Nonpublic-school participation (Educ. § 5-112(g)).** Nonpublic schools may participate in contracts awarded by county boards (or by other public agencies or intergovernmental purchasing organizations) where public-bidding procedures were followed by the lead agency.
- **Sole source / proprietary.** Educ. § 5-112(b) allows manufacturer-specific specifications only when written board determination justifies them (compatibility, student health, replacement-parts/maintenance, or resale); there is no broader pupil-transportation sole-source statute.
- **Vehicle service life (Educ. § 7-804; COMAR 13A.06.07.20).** School vehicles may not be used for more than 12 years (default), with a 15-year cap in 15 enumerated counties (Allegany, Anne Arundel, Calvert, Caroline, Cecil, Charles, Dorchester, Garrett, Kent, Queen Anne's, St. Mary's, Somerset, Talbot, Wicomico, Worcester). Extensions beyond 12 years require approved preventive-maintenance plan with annual MVA inspections, structural-repair certification, specified safety equipment, and State Superintendent approval (§ 7-804(c)). COMAR 13A.06.07.20 bars use after 15 years. This vehicle-age regime is the only state-law parameter that meaningfully drives common contract structures (St. Mary's, Anne Arundel) — boards often peg the contract life to the bus life so contractor capital recovery aligns with the § 7-804 cap.
- **County-superintendent approval (Educ. § 4-205(d)).** 'A contract made by a county board is not valid without the written approval of the county superintendent.' Applies to original award and to any renewal or amendment that creates a new contract instrument.
- **Special education differentiation.** Educ. § 8-410 imposes the duty to provide/arrange transportation for children with disabilities and authorizes MSDE guidelines for (i) hiring/training of drivers/aides (including 'private contract personnel'), and (ii) length/distance of trips to nonpublic special-education facilities, but does not set separate contracting cycle, term, or rebid rules. Annual local-system reports are required for systems with 25+ such children. COMAR 13A.06.07.17 separately governs taxicab drivers transporting students with disabilities (often the contracting vehicle for individual-student SPED transports).
- **Federal-funded transportation duty (not a state contract rule).** Federal IDEA and McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act obligations create duty-to-transport requirements but do not displace the § 5-112 procurement regime; foster-care transportation collaboration with child-welfare agencies is procured under § 5-112.
- **Charter-school rules.** Maryland's charter-school statute (Educ. § 9-101 et seq.) treats public charter schools as public schools within the granting county board and does not establish a separate transportation-contract regime; the granting county board's § 5-112 procurement framework controls.
- **Maryland General Procurement Law (State Fin. & Proc. Div. II) does NOT govern local school-board procurement.** § 11-202 limits Division II to 'expenditure[s] by a unit' (State units), and accordingly the Title 13 source-selection rules govern State agencies, not county boards. County-board pupil-transportation procurement is governed by Educ. § 5-112 (and incorporated thresholds), each board's own procurement manual, and local-school-system policies adopted under COMAR 13A.06.07.
- **2025 legislative activity (NOT enacted) and 2026 refile.** Maryland 2025 SB 727 and HB 1359 (Charles County only) would have required the Charles County Board of Education to provide 10-year advance written notice and to hold a public hearing 5 years in advance of any action displacing a contracted student-bus-transportation provider. Both remained in committee at the close of the 2025 Session. The 2026 Session refile (SB 574 / HB 1493) is similarly pending in committee as of 2026-06-23 (Senate hearing scheduled for 3/11/2026; effective date IF enacted: July 1, 2026). None of these bills is current Maryland law as of 2026-06-23.
- **2026 SB 292 / HB 0319 (enacted Ch. 682, approved May 26, 2026, effective June 1, 2026).** Repeals the sunset on certain provisions authorizing county boards to use vehicles other than school buses, and requires MSDE to study current regulations for non-school-bus vehicles and report by January 1, 2027. Does NOT impose any contract-term, rebid, or renewal requirement on pupil-transportation contracts; included here for currentness.
- **Charles County local context.** The Sept. 1, 2023 Final Report of the Charles County Task Force (Ch. 790, 2023 General Assembly) documents that CCPS historically used annual contracts (a unique outlier in Maryland), and that CCPS and CCSBCA agreed on Aug. 22, 2023 to work toward a multi-year master operating contract along with a 5% COLA and 8-hour minimum workday for contracted bus operators. CCPS has since posted a 'School Bus Multi-Year Contract 2025-26' framework.
- **Recent enforcement (MCPS electric-bus matter, Nov. 2025).** The Maryland State Board of Education denied MCPS's appeal and held the $168M Highland Electric Fleets school-bus contract 'arbitrary, unreasonable, and in violation' of MCPS's own bidding procedures — confirming that under § 5-112 each local board's compliance with its own competitively bid procurement rules is administratively reviewable, and that violations can void contracts under § 5-112(i).
