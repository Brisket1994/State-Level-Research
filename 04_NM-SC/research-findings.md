## Executive Summary

This document records the current law, as of June 24, 2026, governing contracts for student-transportation services (including school-bus contracts) between public school districts and private contractors, for ten states (New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina) across five fields: maximum contract term, whether rebidding is required at term-end, renewal mechanics, statutory citation, and district-level interpretation variance. The posture is that of a neutral recorder of law: the document states what each state's governing authority says without evaluating whether any framework is favorable or unfavorable to any party. Each state was researched independently as its own lane and then adversarially validated against its own sources; no cross-state legal inference was used and no favorability judgments are recorded. A documented silence, "not found," or "Unknown" in any cell is reported as such and is not converted into a confirmed positive rule.

### Coverage & verify-and-converge
- Fields fully resolved vs. documented-silent/not-found/unknown: all 50 field-answers across the ten states (10 states × 5 fields) are populated. Documented silence and documented "Unknown" are themselves recorded results under the neutral-recorder posture, not gaps.

## Notes / Special Provisions

### New Mexico
- The Procurement Code is categorically inapplicable: NMSA 1978, § 13-1-98(H) exempts "contracts with businesses for public school transportation services" from the entire Procurement Code, so § 13-1-102's competitive-bidding mandate and § 13-1-150's general multi-term cap do not control school bus service contracts; the school-specific 5-year-plus-annual-renewal regime in § 22-16-3 governs.
- All school bus service contracts must be written on PED-approved forms (NMSA 1978, § 22-16-3(B); 6.43.2.8(C), 6.43.2.13 (fleet form), 6.43.2.14 (individual owner-operator form) NMAC); PED reviews proposals/awards and may audit local board procurement records (6.43.2.11(H) NMAC).
- For NEW contracts (not renewals), 6.43.2.11 NMAC requires an RFP with ten-day published notice and board-developed evaluation criteria with award to the most efficient/economical/safe proposal; renewals under 6.43.2.12 NMAC require a current financial statement, ownership disclosure, board evaluation of services, and PED notice on approved forms.
- Sole-source / emergency procurement carve-backs were searched for and not located in the school bus framework; § 13-1-98(H)'s exemption is categorical.
- Cooperative purchasing / piggyback: the categorical Procurement Code exemption removes the need for these mechanisms in school bus service contracting; not separately addressed in 6.43.2 NMAC.
- Charter schools: not separately addressed in 6.43.2 NMAC for transportation contract term/renewal.
- Special-education differences: None of substance. 6.31.2 NMAC (special education) and 6.41.4 NMAC (transportation operations) contain no separate term cap or procurement rule for special-ed transportation contracts; NMSBA Sample Policy EEAB references 6.41.4 NMAC and federal IDEA only and contains no provisions on contract term limits or procurement procedures.
- State-approval requirements: Renewal procedures require notice to the state transportation director on PED-approved forms (6.43.2.12(G) NMAC); the renewal is at the local school board's option.
- Rate framework: Under § 22-16-3(C) and 6.43.2.8(D) NMAC, the renewal rate framework must reflect fuel costs, operation and maintenance costs, and (for fleet contractors) employee salary and benefits costs; no statutory CPI/index escalator.

### New York
- Small-purchase/threshold mechanics: GML § 103's purchase-contract bidding threshold is $20,000; contracts at or below that threshold are not subject to competitive bidding via that statute, and Ed. Law § 305(14)(a) cross-references GML § 103 to determine when competitive bidding is required.
- Emergency contracts: Ed. Law § 305(14)(b) allows emergency interim contracts of up to one month without bidding "for any emergency arising out of an accident or other unforeseen occurrence."
- Cooperative/piggyback: Ed. Law § 305(14)(g) permits a district to piggyback on another district's existing private-contractor contract notwithstanding GML § 103 where the board adopts a resolution finding cost-appropriateness and cost savings; NYSED's Piggyback Contract Guidance (Revised 2023) confirms "[a] piggyback contract differs in that it is not subject to the $20,000 aggregate threshold or bidding requirements pursuant to General Municipal Law §103."
- Safety/legal-compliance amendments: Ed. Law § 305(14)(d) permits amendments to active contracts for safety/legal compliance, subject to commissioner approval; provision scheduled for repeal January 1, 2028.
- Charter-school rules: not separately addressed by Ed. Law § 305(14)'s contract framework in this lane.
- Special-education differences: SPED contracts use the SAME procurement framework (Ed. Law § 305(14) and 8 NYCRR Part 156) — only the advertisement-for-bids and RFP-solicitation deadline differs (June 1 for general transportation; July 1 for children-with-disabilities transportation per 8 NYCRR §§ 156.1 and 156.12); Ed. Law § 4402(2)(4) authorizes contracting with municipalities, BOCES, public authorities, or private contractors meeting § 3623 standards.
- State-approval requirements: Each extension is filed on a commissioner-prescribed form and is subject to commissioner approval (8 NYCRR § 156.5(a); Ed. Law § 305(14)(a)).
- CPI mechanics: 8 NYCRR § 156.5(c) caps the CPI window at the 12-month period ending May 31 immediately preceding commencement of the extension; the regional CPI-U is the N.Y., N.Y.-Northeastern, N.J. index.
- District-of-practical-application detail: NYC operates under a Court-of-Appeals-litigated EPP barrier; Comptroller materials and Chalkbeat (Nov. 20, 2025) confirm 46-year-old (1979-era) contracts and the November 2025 ~3-year extension.

### North Carolina
- Federal Uniform Guidance overlay: 2 CFR §§ 200.317–200.327 imposes federal competitive-procurement requirements when the pupil-transportation contract is paid with federal funds; this is a federal grant-compliance overlay, not a state-law rebidding requirement.
- Bus purchases (not services): 16 NCAC 06B .0112 (effective Dec. 1, 2020) requires LEAs to purchase BUSES through statewide term contracts under G.S. 143-49 — that is a vehicle-purchase rule, not a services-contract rule.
- Small-purchase/dollar threshold for bidding: G.S. 143-129's formal-bid threshold is $500,000 (construction/repair) and $90,000 (apparatus/supplies/materials/equipment); G.S. 143-131's informal-bid range is $30,000 to the formal-bid threshold; neither reaches services.
- Sole-source/emergency/cooperative-purchasing: not specifically reached because the underlying bidding statutes do not extend to services.
- Charter-school rules: charter schools are not separately addressed by G.S. 115C-253; G.S. 115C-242.1(b)(2) (automated school bus safety camera service contracts) has a 3-year + one 3-year-renewal cap that does not apply to pupil-transportation contracts.
- Special-education differences: None of substance — G.S. 115C-250 authorizes expenditures for transportation of children with disabilities without setting contract rules; 16 NCAC 06B .0109 (Contract Transportation of Exceptional Children) was emergency-adopted Aug. 20, 2019 and the emergency adoption EXPIRED Dec. 6, 2019, so it is not currently effective; federal IDEA and McKinney-Vento create duty-to-transport obligations only.
- State-approval requirements for renewal: none in the state framework.
- Practical application: Wake County PSS's RFP recites that "this contract for transportation services is not required to be bid under North Carolina law" but uses an RFP to facilitate competition.

### North Dakota
- Lowest-responsible-bidder rule: § 15.1-30-12(1) requires the board to award the contract to the lowest responsible bidder "except as otherwise provided in section 15.1-30-11."
- Limited emergency provision: § 15.1-30-09 permits limited emergency vehicle/operator substitutions but does not waive bidding or the renewal procedure.
- General school-district sealed-bid statute: § 15.1-09-34 generally requires sealed bids for school-district contracts over $50,000 with ten days' published notice, but § 15.1-09-34(1)(g) expressly exempts "School transportation services purchased under section 15.1-30-11" — reinforcing that ch. 15.1-30 is the exclusive contracting framework.
- State procurement code: N.D.C.C. ch. 54-44.4 (State Purchasing Practices) applies to "all state agencies and institutions in the executive branch of state government" and does not reach school districts.
- Sole-source / cooperative-purchasing / piggyback / board-waiver carve-outs were searched for in ch. 15.1-30 and not located.
- DPI standard contract template: § 15.1-30-07 authorizes a state-published "Student Transportation Sample Agreement" — DPI describes it as "a template with suggested areas and language, not an all-inclusive final contract."
- Special-education differences: None of substance — § 15.1-32-16 sets a duty to provide special-education transportation by any reasonably prudent means (regular bus, public transit, commercial transportation, chartered/contracted transportation, or parent-provided transportation) without imposing separate contracting rules; when SPED transportation is contracted from a private provider, ch. 15.1-30 governs.
- Charter schools: not separately addressed in ch. 15.1-30.

### Ohio
- Multi-year structuring: districts may enter multi-year contracts under ORC 5705.41 and 5705.44, typically "subject to non-appropriation," with the fiscal officer certifying funds annually; unfulfilled obligations from prior years are carried as fixed charges in the next year's appropriation (ORC 5705.44).
- Cooperative purchasing alternatives: ORC 9.48 (eff. Sept. 12, 2008) authorizes joint purchasing programs; ORC 125.04 (eff. Sept. 30, 2021) authorizes participation in the DAS Cooperative Purchasing Program; 2024 Op. Atty.Gen. 2024-003 limits cooperative purchasing for construction services but permits it for non-construction installation, maintenance, and repair services.
- Small-purchase / bidding threshold: ORC 9.17 sets an initial $75,000 threshold with a 3% annual escalator; current applied threshold is $77,250 through Dec. 31, 2025 per Ohio Auditor of State Bulletin 2025-009.
- HB 96 (136th GA, eff. Sept. 30, 2025) clarified ORC 3313.46 by replacing "any school building" with "any building or other property," broadening the bidding mandate to non-instructional buildings but leaving services contracts outside the bidding requirement; HB 96 also added a Montgomery County Pupil Transportation Pilot Program and a community-school transportation pilot, and proposed a student-transportation workgroup (R.C. 3327.18 / Section 733.80) that was not yet codified at codes.ohio.gov as of 2026-06-24.
- ORC 3327.012's payment cap (state Department-level contracts at the state average cost per pupil) is a payment cap on state-agency contracts only, not a district contract term cap.
- Board contracting and authorization: ORC 3313.17 establishes the board as a body politic and corporate with general contracting authority; ORC 3313.33 requires contracts to be authorized at a regular or special meeting.
- Special-education differences: ORC 3323.08 permits contracts with nonpublic entities for special education and related services but imposes no term/bidding/renewal rule; OAC Chapter 3301-83 contains no rule on contract term, competitive bidding, or renewal mechanics for private transportation contractors.
- District examples (variance evidence): Valley View FY2026 (10 + five 1-year options); West Clermont 2021–2022 (3 + 3); Lakota Local SD 2025 (RFP recites Section 3313.46 non-applicability); Talawanda CSD 2020 performance audit (incumbent negotiated; Recommendation 8 to align with peer districts).

### Oklahoma
- Multi-year structuring: every multi-year arrangement is functionally a one-year primary term followed by affirmative mutual ratification of renewal each subsequent fiscal year (2005 OK AG 14); non-appropriation clauses are not authorized for school-district contracts.
- 62 O.S. § 430.1 codifies the mutual-ratification rule specifically for school-district rental of real or personal property extending beyond June 30 (construed in 2005 OK AG 12 (Garrison) in the lease-purchase-of-roof context); 2005 OK AG 14 articulates the broader rule for school-district services contracts derived from Okla. Const. art. X § 26(a).
- Bus purchases (not services): 70 O.S. § 9-104 and DPS guidance require school-bus PURCHASES to use the State contract or sealed-bid method.
- Sole-source / emergency / cooperative-purchasing carve-outs were searched for in ch. 70 Art. IX and not located beyond the limited emergency-vehicle/operator authorities; the Central Purchasing Act (74 O.S. § 85) does not reach school districts.
- The general Public Competitive Bidding Act (61 O.S. §§ 101–138) applies to "public construction contracts" >$100,000 only; "public improvement" expressly excludes personal property and supplies.
- Special-education differences: None of substance — OAC 210:30-5-3(d) imposes a duty to provide transportation as an IDEA related service but is silent on contract term/bidding/renewal; no separate Oklahoma special-education transportation procurement statute located.
- State-board approval: 70 O.S. § 9-105 requires state-board approval of transportation-area boundary determinations, not contract approval.
- 2025 session: HB 1244 addresses passenger-bus seat-belt requirements only; it does not change the contracting framework.

### Oregon
- Local Contract Review Boards: ORS 279A.060 — if the governing body of a local contracting agency takes no action otherwise, the governing body is the LCRB.
- Opt-out from AG Model Rules: ORS 279A.065(5)–(6) — Model Rules apply if no own-rule adopted; agency may adopt own rules that specifically displace the Model Rules and prescribe procedure.
- Small/intermediate procurement thresholds: ORS 279B.065 ≤ $25,000 small procurements; ORS 279B.070 > $25,000 and ≤ $250,000 intermediate procurements (effective Jan. 1, 2024 thresholds; no effect on term/renewal/rebid timing).
- Sole-source / emergency / special procurements: ORS 279B.075/.080/.085 statutory exemptions to the competitive default.
- Cooperative procurements: ORS 279A.200–.225 permit cooperative procurement as an alternative path.
- ORS 332.075 grants the district school board power to contract and to authorize the superintendent to enter into and approve payment on contracts, with express carve-outs for collective bargaining agreements and "service contracts that include the provision of labor performed by employees," both of which remain with the board.
- Pupil-transportation operational/safety rules: OAR 581-053-0004 — "Transportation entities that contract out all or part of their pupil transportation services are required to ensure that their contractor complies with all applicable laws and administrative rules." Safety/operational compliance only; no contract-term or procurement rule.
- Cost-accounting: OAR 581-023-0040 governs Approved Transportation Costs for State School Fund payments; not a procurement rule.
- Special-education differences: ORS 343.221 authorizes districts to contract for special education with another district, ESD, approved private agency, or credentialed individual; silent on contract term and procurement procedure.
- District examples (variance evidence): Beaverton SD policy DJC (Board acts as LCRB; cites ORS 279/279A/B/C and OAR Ch. 137 Divs. 045-049 and OAR Ch. 125 Divs. 246-249); Reynolds SD DJC; Roseburg PS Contracting Rules (own-rule under ORS 279A.065).

### Pennsylvania
- COVID-19 emergency renegotiation: 24 P.S. § 15-1501.8 (added by Act 13 of 2020) authorized continued payment to school bus transportation contractors during pandemic closure and permitted "each school entity [to] renegotiate a contract for school bus transportation services to ensure contracted personnel and fixed costs … are maintained during the period of school closure" — a one-time provision, not a general renewal rule.
- DOE approval: 24 P.S. § 13-1365 requires transportation contracts to be filed with and approved by the Department of Education; 24 P.S. § 25-2541 conditions state reimbursement on DOE-approved means and contracts; 22 Pa. Code § 23.35 addresses reimbursement formula only.
- General school-board bidding statute: 24 P.S. § 8-807.1 reaches only "furniture, equipment, textbooks, school supplies and other appliances" — transportation services are not within its categories.
- Commonwealth Procurement Code: 62 Pa.C.S. § 102 generally excludes political subdivisions/school districts (except per Chapter 19 cooperative purchasing).
- Auditor General audits (2013–2016): 19 school districts identified as not bidding bus contracts, paying approximately $54.8–54.9 million more than the state reimbursement formula would have indicated; districts highlighted include Scranton SD, Penn Hills SD, Monessen SD, Riverview SD, and Crestwood SD.

- District-policy examples: Philadelphia SD Policy 810 (rev. June 25, 2020) delegates contracting authority to the Superintendent and cross-references Policy 610 (Purchases Subject to Competitive Process) and Policy 611.

### Rhode Island
- Statewide system: § 16-21.1-8 establishes a statewide transportation system; RIDE's October 9, 2024 Statewide Transportation System Overview notes the system currently handles out-of-district special-ed routes, homeless transportation, and foster-care transportation, with management by Transpar Group and services by First Student and Ocean State Transit.
- Joint purchasing: § 16-2-9.2 permits two or more school committees to establish joint purchasing agreements and cross-references § 37-2-56 for piggyback on state master price agreements; § 45-55-13.3 expressly exempts § 16-2-9.2 consortia from Title 45 Chapter 55.
- Substantive renewal conditions under § 16-21-1: (c) statewide-system participation; (d) 180-day personnel-payment requirement; (e) electric-bus preference (equal-weight with other vendor preferences); (f) CBA-respecting discipline procedures preserved on extension/renewal.

### South Carolina
- Statewide context: SC school bus transportation is predominantly delivered through a state-owned/leased bus fleet under SBE/SCDE authority (§§ 59-67-410, 59-67-420; SBE Reg. 43-80), so private district-level contracting under § 59-67-460 historically occurs as supplemental, special-needs, or alternative service.
- Multi-year structuring and annual appropriations: § 11-35-2030(1)–(3) — solicitation must disclose term and renewal/extension conditions; payment and performance obligations for succeeding fiscal periods subject to availability and appropriation; the governmental body must determine in writing that estimated requirements cover the period and are reasonably firm and continuing and that the contract serves the State's interest by encouraging effective competition or otherwise promoting economies; cancellation required if funds are not appropriated.
- Source-selection alternatives: § 11-35-1510 lists sixteen alternatives to competitive sealed bidding (small purchase, sole source, emergency, cooperative purchasing under Article 19, and others) that may be used in lieu of sealed bidding under § 11-35-1520.
- Applicability to school districts: § 11-35-310(18) excludes school districts from "Governmental body"; § 11-35-310(24) includes school districts in "Political subdivision"; § 11-35-5340 subjects a district to the Code when annual expenditures exceed $75 million unless the district has an SCSFAA-approved substantially-similar own code; § 11-35-5320 requires all political subdivisions to adopt procurement laws but provides that "[a] political subdivision's failure to adopt … is not subject to the legal remedies provided in this code."
- Model SDPC: SCSFAA Division of Procurement Services has published a 2021 Model School District Procurement Code under Reg. 19-445.3000(E); the Model substitutes "Superintendent" for "chief procurement officer" in § 2030(4) and "board" (i.e., the school board) for SCSFAA in § 2030(5).
- Pending legislation (NOT current law): S.C. H.3242 (2025-2026, "South Carolina School Bus Privatization Act of 2025") would require school districts beginning 2026-2027 (and no later than 2028-2029) to prepare and publish RFPs for school transportation services if they elect not to provide such services themselves; bill remains in House Ways and Means and is NOT enacted as of 2026-06-24; the bill does not specify a contract-term cap.
- Special-education differences: None of substance — § 59-67-520 imposes a duty on the State Department of Education to transport handicapped persons; SBE Reg. 43-80 § XXV.B addresses individual parent/contract transportation for hard-to-reach students (per-mile reimbursement, 90-school-day periodicity), not large-scale private-bus-contractor procurement.
