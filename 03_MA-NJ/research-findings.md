# Student Transportation Contract Rules — Set 3 (MA–NJ): Research Findings

**As-of date:** 2026-06-24
**Scope:** Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Jersey.

## Executive Summary

This deliverable documents current state law, as of 2026-06-24, on the rules that govern contracts between public school districts and private student-transportation contractors in the ten MA–NJ states, across five fields: maximum contract term, rebid-at-term-end, renewal mechanics, statutory citation, and district-level interpretation variance. Each state was researched independently by a dedicated research lane and then adversarially validated by a separate lane that re-opened every cited source and challenged every field. The deliverables are written from a neutral-recorder posture: they state what the governing authority says, not whether any state's framework is favorable or unfavorable to any party, and they make no cross-state legal inference (no state's rule was used to fill a gap in another's).

## Notes / Special Provisions

### Massachusetts
- **Small-purchase threshold:** Under M.G.L. c.30B § 4, small-purchase procedures apply only below the $50,000 sealed-bidding threshold; school transportation contracts virtually always exceed this.
- **Sole source / emergency:** Sole source under § 7 requires written justification (cap $50,000 generally, $100,000 for municipalities/regional school districts); emergency under § 8 is short-term.
- **Cooperative / piggyback:** § 22 authorizes cooperative purchasing; the underlying solicitation conducted by the cooperative must still use full and open competition.
- **Charter-school rules:** Horace Mann charter schools (part of a public school district) are subject to Ch. 30B; Commonwealth charter schools have separate procurement obligations under different statutes but are largely subject to similar competitive requirements.
- **Special education:** M.G.L. c.30B § 1(b)(8) exempts agreements for the provision of special education pursuant to c.71B (and regulations) from Ch. 30B. The OIG Chapter 30B Manual (9th ed., ch. 13, exemption #8) confirms this exemption "applies to direct services delivered to children with special needs, such as transportation, counseling or education under an individual education program." The Mass. OIG's February 2026 Special Education Transportation Study reaffirms this exemption.
- **State-approval requirements:** For pupil-transportation contracts seeking state reimbursement, the OIG Chapter 30B Manual states: "Under M.G.L. c.71, §7A, you must use an IFB process to procure school bus transportation contracts for which you are seeking state reimbursement."
- **Annual appropriation:** Each contract is subject to annual appropriation; § 12(d) requires cancellation if funds are not appropriated.
- **Citation correction adopted in validation:** Stage 1's secondary citation to M.G.L. c.71B § 14 is OUTDATED (repealed 2019 by St. 2019, c. 132, § 20); the c.30B § 1(b)(8) special-education exemption analysis is unaffected.

### Michigan
- **Small-purchase / thresholds:** State competitive-bidding thresholds in MCL 380.1267 (construction), MCL 380.1274 (supplies/materials/equipment for school districts/PSAs), and MCL 380.623a (ISD supplies/materials/equipment) are each $30,512 for FY 2024-25 per the MDE Oct. 10, 2024 threshold memo; transportation services are not within the enumerated scope of any of these statutes.
- **Sole-source / emergency:** Not addressed by state statute for transportation services; left to local board policy and contract drafting.
- **Cooperative / piggyback:** No statutory carve-out specific to transportation services.
- **Charter-school rules:** Public school academies (charter schools) are within MCL 380.1274's scope for supplies/materials/equipment but not for transportation services contracts.
- **Special education:** MCL 380.1751 requires special-education contracts to include specified content (buildings, equipment, personnel, transportation per §§ 380.1756, 380.1757, the sending district's financial contribution) but imposes no max term, competitive-bidding rule, or renewal mechanic specific to special-education transportation contracts. MARSE (Mich. Admin. Code R 340.1701-340.1873), including R 340.1810 (reimbursement of special-education transportation), governs reimbursement, not contract procurement.
- **PERA / outsourcing — citation correction adopted in validation:** Stage 1's reliance on MCL 423.215(3)(f) (prohibition on bargaining over outsourcing) was REFUTED. 2023 PA 115 and 2023 PA 143 (both eff. Feb. 13, 2024) repealed the prior prohibition; the current MCL 423.215(3)(f) addresses "the use of volunteers in providing services at its schools." The decision to outsource noninstructional services (e.g., transportation) is now a permissible (not prohibited) subject of bargaining; the union must be given the opportunity to bid on an equal basis as other bidders (per MSBO guidance). None of this creates a state-law competitive-bidding mandate on the transportation-services contract itself.
- **Federal overlay:** When federal funds (e.g., IDEA flow-through, ESSER, Food Service) pay for or support the contract, federal Uniform Guidance procurement standards (2 CFR Part 200) attach.

### Minnesota
- **Small-purchase threshold:** Minn. Stat. § 471.345, subd. 3, sets the general municipal sealed-bid floor at $175,000, with $25,001–$175,000 sealed-bid-or-two-quotations and $25,000-or-less open-market options. For school-district transportation contracts, § 123B.52, subd. 3 supplies the controlling procurement framework "notwithstanding ... section 471.345."
- **Sole-source / emergency:** Not specifically addressed in § 123B.52, subd. 3 for transportation contracts; subd. 3's three permitted procurement methods remain mandatory.
- **Cooperative / piggyback:** Minn. Stat. § 471.345, subd. 15 authorizes use of state cooperative-purchasing contracts and national municipal-alliance arrangements without standard competitive bidding for municipal contracts, but this does NOT displace § 123B.52, subd. 3 for transportation contracts.
- **Charter-school rules:** Not separately addressed in the transportation-procurement framework; LEAs are directed to follow § 471.345 and § 123B.52.
- **Special education:** Minn. Stat. ch. 125A (Special Education) and Minn. R. ch. 3525 (Special Education) contain no separate SPED-transportation contract-term/bidding/renewal regime. The MDE Special Education Funding Reference Guide, Section 26 (Procurement), directs LEAs to follow § 471.345 and § 123B.52 for procurement; no separate carve-out.
- **State-approval requirements:** None at the state level for SPED transportation procurement under § 123B.52, subd. 3.

### Mississippi
- **Small-purchase / dollar thresholds:** § 31-7-13(c) is cross-referenced only for the newspaper-advertisement procedure for sealed bids; the general purchasing thresholds and sole-source/emergency carve-outs in § 31-7-13 do not displace the school-specific bid/extension rule in § 37-41-31.
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement:** Not addressed by § 37-41-31; the only express negotiation-without-bidding pathway is § 37-41-31(2) for exceptional-children contracts or extraordinary circumstances where regular transportation is impractical.
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority:** No statutory carve-out specific to school-bus contracts; the school-specific § 37-41-31 controls.
- **Charter-school rules:** Not separately addressed in the transportation-contract framework.
- **Special education:** § 37-41-31(2) permits negotiated procurement (no bid) for exceptional-children transportation; § 37-41-29(4) exempts such contracts from State Board filing. The statute does NOT specify a different maximum term for exceptional-children contracts; the 4 + 4 cap in § 37-41-31(3) appears in a separate paragraph that is not expressly limited to bid-let contracts and is not expressly extended to negotiated exceptional-children contracts (this is unresolved on the face of the statute and no AG opinion or MDE bulletin clarifying it was located).
- **State-approval / filing requirements:** § 37-41-29(3) requires a written contract to be filed with the superintendent of schools (general rule); § 37-41-29(4) exempts exceptional-children and extraordinary-circumstances contracts from State Board of Education filing.
- **Per-pupil funding cap:** § 37-41-29(2) caps per-pupil state funding for private-contract transportation at the amount allowed per pupil for public transportation in the same county and district.

### Missouri
- **Small-purchase thresholds:** No state-mandated dollar threshold for school-district services; thresholds vary by district policy (Auditor 2006-43 observed $100 to $5,000 in sampled districts).
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement:** Not addressed by state statute for school-district services; left to local board policy (e.g., MSBA DJF-AP1 sections 2 and 6).
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority:** RSMo 70.220 (intergovernmental cooperation) is permissive only and does not impose a transportation-contracting regime.
- **Charter-school rules:** Not separately addressed in the transportation-contracting framework.
- **Special education:** RSMo 162.710 imposes the duty to provide special-education transportation (including to contracted day classes) but is not a contracting/bidding/renewal rule. 5 CSR 30-261.040(1)(G) and (2)(A) govern allowable costs for state transportation aid (including special-education), again not contracting rules.
- **State-approval requirements:** None for the transportation contract itself; 5 CSR 30-261.010(1)(D) requires the contract to be on file at the district.
- **2019 amendment to RSMo 177.086:** Raised the school-construction competitive-bidding threshold from $15,000 to $50,000 (no change to scope; transportation services remain outside).

### Montana
- **Small-purchase / dollar threshold:** Not separately addressed for school-bus contracts; MCA 20-10-125 applies regardless of amount.
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement:** Not located in MCA Title 20, Ch. 10, Part 1 or ARM Ch. 10.7. The Montana Procurement Act (Title 18, Ch. 4) and its sole-source/emergency exceptions are not automatically available to school districts because MCA 18-4-124(1) makes the Procurement Act optional for school districts; a district would have to have affirmatively adopted those provisions.
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority:** MCA 20-10-126 addresses transportation service-area cooperative agreements (valid for current school year) — not a carve-out from the MCA 20-10-125 bid/12% rule for the underlying private-contractor contract.
- **Charter-school rules:** Not separately addressed in the transportation-contract framework.
- **Special education:** No separate MT statutory or administrative procurement framework for SPED transportation. MCA Title 20, Ch. 10, Part 1 and ARM Ch. 10.7 apply uniformly. ARM Chapter 10.16 (Special Education) addresses identification, IEPs, due-process, tuition rates, records — not procurement. ARM 37.86.2234 (Medicaid EPSDT specialized transportation) is a Medicaid coverage/billing rule, not procurement. ARM 10.61.207 addresses Montana School for the Deaf and Blind residential students only.
- **State-approval requirements:** None directly on contract awards; OPI publishes uniform TR-1/TR-4/TR-5 reporting forms for statewide reporting.
- **Timing:** Bus contracts must be signed prior to trustees' adoption of the budget; transportation contracts for the ensuing year must be completed by the fourth Monday of June; individual transportation contracts (parent/guardian) signed by July 1 (ARM 10.7.105).

### Nebraska
- **Small-purchase threshold:** None state-imposed for transportation services; districts set thresholds via local policy (e.g., OPS Board Policy 7000 sets $50,000 as the sealed-bid/RFP threshold).
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement:** Not specifically addressed by state statute for transportation services; left to local board policy.
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority:** Not specifically addressed by state statute for transportation services; districts may use cooperative arrangements under general authority.
- **Charter-school rules:** Nebraska does not have charter schools.
- **Special education:** 92 NAC ch. 51 (Rule 51) treats transportation as a "related service" (§ 003.49) and addresses Level III Special Education Contractual Services rate-approval (§ 003.37); does not prescribe a separate maximum term, rebid rule, or renewal mechanism. The § 79-515 seven-year cap applies generally.
- **State-approval requirements:** None for transportation services contracts.
- **Court annotation:** Central City Ed. Assn. v. Merrick Cty. Sch. ### Nevada
- **Small-purchase threshold:** Under NRS 332.063, the threshold for solicitation of two-or-more responses (advertisement permissive) is "more than $50,000 but not more than $100,000"; under NRS 332.065, advertisement is mandatory for contracts over $100,000.
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement:** NRS 332.112 (emergencies); NRS 332.115 (sole-source/professional services and other enumerated exceptions, including equipment maintenance, compatible equipment, perishable goods, insurance, computer hardware/software, books, undercover-vehicle fuel, forensic equipment, personal safety equipment).
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority:** NRS 332.195 permits joinder on solicitations conducted by other governmental entities or cooperative purchasing organizations (subject to a Chapter 624 contractor-license carve-out).
- **Charter-school rules:** Not separately addressed in the transportation-contracting framework.
- **Special education:** NRS 386.790(1) expressly folds pupils with disabilities into the same trustee transportation authority used for regular-route students. No separate procurement statute or NAC chapter for special-ed transportation contracting was located. NAC 386 (school bus safety standards) does not impose separate procurement rules.
- **Carrier exception:** NRS 332.135(1) preserves authority to contract with certificated common or contract carriers at officially approved tariff rates; NRS 386.800(2)(a) confirms that pupil-transportation carriers may hold a certificate of public convenience and necessity issued by the Nevada Transportation Authority — bringing such carriers within NRS 332.135.
- **2025 session amendment:** AB 533 (2025) amended NRS 386.790 (effective July 1, 2026) to direct NDE to administer a grant program for out-of-zone pupil transportation; does NOT add contract-term, rebid, or renewal rules.

### New Hampshire
- **Small-purchase threshold:** None state-imposed for school district contracts; per NHMA, "as a general rule, there is no state law requiring competitive bidding for town contracts and purchases unless a public official is involved as one of the sellers or contractors."
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement:** Not addressed by state statute for transportation services; left to local board policy.
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority:** Not specifically addressed at the state level for school transportation. SB580 (2026), which would have created a state cooperative purchasing program including student transportation, was voted Inexpedient to Legislate by the House on 2026-04-23 (not enacted).
- **Charter-school rules:** Not separately addressed in the transportation-contracting framework.
- **Special education:** NH Code Admin. Rules Ed 1109.02 addresses only vehicle safety/inspection/licensing (Saf-C 1307 / RSA 266:7) and driver licensing (Saf-C 1304), with a compliance-with-(a)-(b) requirement for contracted providers. No term/bidding/renewal provisions. RSA 189:6-f, III addresses special-needs eligibility for school transportation vehicles per IEP/Section 504 plans, again without addressing procurement.
- **Annual-appropriations / multi-year contracts:** RSA 32:7 (lapse of appropriations) and RSA 32:13 (effect of appropriations on prior contractual obligations) frame multi-year obligations as either escape-clause (non-appropriation) contracts (approved by simple majority) or binding multi-year obligations (potentially requiring special warrant-article authorization).
- **State-approval requirements:** None at the state level for school-district transportation contracts.

### New Jersey
- **Small-purchase / quoted-contract threshold:** $24,200 effective 7/1/2025-6/30/2027 for quoted/parental contracts; the formal bid threshold for full pupil-transportation contracts is established under N.J.S.A. 18A:39-3 and adjusted by the Governor each odd-numbered year. N.J.A.C. 6A:27-9.12 addresses quoted contracts below the threshold.
- **Sole-source / emergency procurement:** The Chapter 39 framework is the operative procurement regime; the Public School Contracts Law sole-source/emergency provisions are excluded from pupil-transportation contracts via N.J.S.A. 18A:18A-49.1.
- **Cooperative / piggyback authority:** Joint transportation agreements (N.J.A.C. 6A:27-9.16) and CTSA agreements (N.J.A.C. 6A:27-10) are authorized; CTSA agreements are awarded by resolution rather than competitive bid.
- **Charter-school rules:** Not separately addressed in the Chapter 39 framework documented above; resident-district responsibilities for charter-school student transportation are governed under separate provisions of N.J.S.A. 18A:39.
- **Special education:** Same Chapter 39 / N.J.A.C. 6A:27 contracting framework applies. Operational differences: (i) per N.J.A.C. 6A:27-9.3, in-district SPED routes use per-mile adjustment, while out-of-district SPED routes may be bid per-vehicle/per-student/per-mile; routes requiring an aide are bid per-diem; (ii) N.J.A.C. 6A:27-10 (Coordinated Transportation Services) requires resident districts to utilize a CTSA to transport students attending special-education or vocational schools outside the resident district; (iii) parental contracts under N.J.S.A. 18A:39-1.3 are negotiated, not bid; (iv) N.J.S.A. 18A:39-11.2 imposes Chapter 39 bidding/renewal requirements on CTSA/ESC/jointure entities providing transportation to local districts, with narrow grandfather carve-outs.
- **State-approval requirement:** Every pupil-transportation contract above the threshold must be approved by the executive county superintendent (a state officer), per N.J.A.C. 6A:27-9.9. This is the principal mechanism for statewide uniformity.
- **Public School Contracts Law carve-out:** N.J.S.A. 18A:18A-49.1 expressly excludes pupil-transportation contracts from the PSCL — the 5-year general services cap in N.J.S.A. 18A:18A-42 does not control pupil-transportation contracts.
- **Debarment:** P.L.2021, c.307 added N.J.S.A. 18A:39-11.5; annual extensions may not be granted to a debarred contractor.
